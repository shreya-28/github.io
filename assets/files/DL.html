Privacy-preserving Deep Learning using SPDZ

As a summer intern at Nanyang Technological University, Singapore under the supervision of Prof Chaoping Xing, I focused on secure deep learning. In particular, I worked on security of deep learning models, in rings and fields, against an active dishonest majority using the SPDZ protocol.

Privacy of non-linear models had not been explored in the active setting as activation functions either have to be linearly-approximated leading to a loss in the accuracy, or require a large degree which would incur a substantial overhead. My solution to mitigate these issues was using specialised non-linear protocols over certain algebraic-structures that allow for efficient computation of non-linear operations.

We employed this approach on neural nets for the first time, and I presented this work at the AAAI Workshop on Privacy-Preserving Artificial Intelligence 2020 in New York City, which was organised by Georgia Institute of Technology.

For more details, please refer the paper.
