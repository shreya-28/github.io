Secure and Efficient Federated Learning

As an intern at Nanyang Technological University, Singapore, I collaborated with Webank, China to come up with a practical solution for Federated Transfer Learning. My task was to incorporate Secret Sharing to the federated setting, as the only existing solution for the problem was based on an inefficient Homomorphic Encryption based construction.

The federated setting allows clients to train their local models, output weights of this training are then aggregated to update the global model. Hence, this setting circumvents the traditional problems of the private evaluation of the entire model, such as the restrictions on division and exponetiation involved in secure multi-party computation. Instead, the focus has to be on security lapses introduced due to the sharing of intermediate weights.

To this end, I designed and implemented a novel aggregation algorithm for the problem based on additive secret sharing. This algorithm showed 40x improvement in runtime for the semi-honest setting and got published at the IEEE Bigdata Conference 2019 (Los Angeles, CA, USA).
